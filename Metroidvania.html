Metroidvania

Metroidvanias are a subgenre of action-adventure, role-playing video games, with the term being from a combination of the Metroid and Castlevania series, as the games in the genre borrow from both series. They are typically based on 2D, sidescrolling platform gameplay that features a large interconnected world that the player can explore, though certain parts are locked off until the player has acquired special items, weapons, abilities, or knowledge within the game, often these are protected by a boss character providing story-driven challenges. These improvements help players with defeating more difficult enemies, locating shortcuts and secret areas, and often requires the player to backtrack across the map to unlock new areas. Because of this Metroidvania games tend to be more focused on story and level design, with careful design of the levels and controls to encourage exploration and experimenting with new abilities.

The first Metroid game itself came out in 1986 and established the princibles of non-linear platformers, with Super Metroid, which came out in 1994, is considered to have polished the gameplay style that's integral to Metroidvanias. Then in 1997, Castlevania: Symphony of the Night came out, which also incorporated role-playing game elements, it is considered to be the defining Metroidvania game.
